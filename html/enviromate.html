An AI tool

Type: Group work

When: 2023

Duration: Ten weeks, half time

Project scope: Creation of an AI tool for researchers

This project investigates the role of researchers in addressing climate change and explores the potential of Artificial Intelligence (AI) to improve how organizations and researchers operate in this context. The aim was to develop a service that becomes a researcher's ultimate companion, offering support in reducing the time spent writing reports and communicating research findings. Like a best friend, this service understands that researchers want to focus on conducting groundbreaking research rather than getting stuck in communication issues.

EnviroMate

An online tool based on an AI model that enables researchers to input data and swiftly generate content for publication.

What is the idea?

To save time and help climate researchers communicate their findings.

What is its function?

To improve workflow times and researcher's communicating capacities with different types of audiences and within different contexts.

An overview of the AI

Several alternative solutions were studied to address the possibility of using AI to help the researcher communicate their findings in different formats. Through careful evaluation, six key features were chosen to empower researchers to effectively communicate their results to a broader audience. To further clarify the purpose and functionality of the system, the identified features were categorized into three different categories: Writing Assistant, Visualization, and Presentation. These categories contained different features that aimed to assist researchers in various stages of converting their discoveries.

Writing Assistant

In this project, developing an AI writing assistance required implementing a pre-trained model called Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers (BERT). In addition, the model was fine-tuned using datasets to optimize its performance and accuracy.

Visualization

The system also offered a visualization tool, which assisted researchers in communicating their information more effectively. To make these features feasible, a collaboration was formed with the company Flourish to avoid the substantial costs associated with training and developing models for diverse visualizations. Moreover, EnviroMate offered a convenient interface and acted as a reliable guide by suggesting the most suitable visualizations for each occasion. By analyzing the data and the selected target group, EnviroMate provided valuable recommendations tailored to specific needs. A rule-based approach enabled the recommendations using specific guidelines and criteria to determine the appropriate visualizations for different scenarios.

Presentation

EnviroMate also provided a feature for generating scripts for oral speeches and presentation slides. However, since no pre-existing datasets contained scripts and related reports, we needed to create the sets from scratch.
