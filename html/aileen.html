An AI for mammography

Type: Group of two

When: 2024

Duration: 20 weeks

Project scope: Master's thesis on AI and mammography in collaboration with the start-up company Aileen Health.

The project aimed to explore key factors for building trust and motivation among radiologists to integrate AI within the existing mammography workflow and, based on these findings, develop a design proposal for Aileen Health.

The problem

About one-third of breast cancer tumors reach a size where they are noticeable to women, often signifying advanced stages of the disease and complicating treatment. This could be due to the tumor being either rapidly growing or not detected early enough by medical professionals. The AI startup Aileen Health uses generative AI to simulate cancer by creating future medical images that predict when and where breast cancer might occur. This approach aims to make patients more aware of their risk, help radiologists avoid invasive procedures, and help pharma identify treatment responses for patients.

Purpose of the Master's thesis

To ensure successful AI implementation, radiologists must trust and adapt to the new system. This master's thesis investigated how radiologists interact with AI, focusing on optimizing the presentation of AI-generated visualizations to build trust, promote adoption, and facilitate effective usage. The thesis aimed to answer the following research question: What are the key factors for building trust and integrating AI-based predictions into the current mammography screening workflow to support radiologists?

Research question

What are the key factors for building trust and integrating AI-based predictions into the current mammography screening workflow to support radiologists?

The process

Our process began with an extensive literature review to gain a deeper understanding of breast cancer and the current use of AI in this field. We used an iterative design process with five phases: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and test. Furthermore, we conducted interviews and observations with breast radiologists. These insights led to the development of requirements and guidelines for our final design solution.

The result

The design proposal includes three main components: the Future Multiview, the Future AI Overview, and the Landing Page. Together, these elements aim to build radiologists' trust in the AI system and encourage its integration into their workflow to improve patient care.
